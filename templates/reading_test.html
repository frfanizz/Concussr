The King-Devick test is an effective method to test the ocular affects of a concussion. In this test, you are tasked with reading each line of numbers out loud.

There are three different tests, each with their own set of numbers. For each test, you hit the 'start test' button, read the numbers out loud, and click the same button to end the recording when you are done reading the numbers.

After all three tests are done, you will be shown how many numbers you correctly read in order, and how long it took for you to read all of the sets. Taking too long on the tests, or having too many errors, indicates degraded vision processing, which may be indicative of a concussion.
